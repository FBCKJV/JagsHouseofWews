The Epistle to the Romans

The Book of Romans is the longest and most doctrinally significant of Paul's letters. It serves as a systematic explanation of the gospel of grace, written to a church Paul had not yet visited.

Who, When & Where?

  • ✎
    Author: Paul (Scribe: Tertius)
  • ⏰
    When: AD 57–58 (3rd Journey)
  • 🏠
    Where: Written from Corinth

Why Did Paul Write It?

  1. To systematically explain the gospel of grace.
  2. To unify Jewish & Gentile believers in Rome.
  3. To prepare for his upcoming visit.
  4. To gain support for missions to Jerusalem & Spain.

Quick Facts

  • ⭐
    Longest Epistle: 16 chapters, 7,111 words.
  • ⭐
    Only letter to a church Paul didn't plant.
  • ⭐
    Quotes the Old Testament ~60 times.

A Letter Steeped in Scripture

Romans is deeply connected to the Old Testament, with ~60 quotes. Paul draws heavily from foundational texts to build his theological argument for the gospel.

Who is Paul & Why Does He Matter?

The author of Romans is one of the most pivotal figures in history. His life was defined by a dramatic transformation, from a zealous persecutor of the church to its most influential apostle.

Saul of Tarsus: The Persecutor

  • A Pharisee from the Tribe of Benjamin.
  • Zealously persecuted the early church in Jerusalem.
  • Was present at the martyrdom of Stephen (Acts 7).

The Conversion: The Damascus Road

  • Occurred around AD 35.
  • A great light from heaven and the voice of Jesus.
  • Christ's words: "Saul, Saul, why persecutest thou me?"

Paul's Ministry & Legacy

Following his conversion, Paul spent years in preparation before embarking on a ministry that would establish the theological foundations of Christianity and spread the gospel across the Roman Empire.
