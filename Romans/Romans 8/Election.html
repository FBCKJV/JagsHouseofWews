Biblical Election According to the KJV
What the Text Actually Says

Election in Scripture means being singled out by God for a special purpose, privilege, or service.
It is never presented as God choosing who will believe or be caused to be saved.
It is always accompanied by responsibilities and, in many cases, warnings of real consequences for failure.

This presentation simply lets the KJV passages speak in their own context:

God chooses (elects) people/groups for specific purposes, privileges, and service.
The elect are consistently described as already believing, loving God, walking in truth, or (in the case of Israel) already in covenant relation.
Election is always "in Christ" or tied to faith/obedience/sanctification where details are given.
Strong responsibilities and real warnings are attached in passage after passage.

No verse ever says God elects certain individuals to be caused to believe while passing others by.
