WEEK 3: ROMANS 2:1-11

The Inescapable Judgment

1. The Inexcusable Judge (Romans 2:1-4)

Paul is shifting his **diatribe** (a logical argument to persuade) from the Reprobate (Rom 1) to the person who would judge them, using the hypothetical **Interlocutor** ("O man").
  • The Condemnation of the Judge: "Therefore thou art **inexcusable**, O man, whosoever thou art that judgest".
  • You Condemn Yourself: "for wherein thou judgest another, **thou condemnest thyself**".
  • **The Judge Commits the Same Sins:** "for thou that judgest **doest the same things**".
  • **The Concept of Wrath for Sin:** Paul used the **extreme case of Reprobates** to establish the concept of wrath for sin. Recognizing the wrath of God against the reprobates means **we who do any of the same are just as guilty**.
  • **All are Accountable:** Paul established that even **heathens know enough about God to be held accountable**.
  • **God is Just:** "We are sure that the **judgment of God is according to truth** against them which commit such things".

2. The Treasury of Wrath (Romans 2:5-6)

Paul is introducing a concept of judgment similar to **accounting or bookkeeping**. The sinner who persists in sin is actively building a record with God, not escaping.

  • **You Cannot Escape:** "If you think you aren’t subject to the same wrath as the worst reprobate, you are wrong. **None shall escape the judgment of God**".
  • **The Sinner Builds an Account:** The sinner "builds up an account with God".
  • **Storing Up Wrath:** "Thou... **treasurest up unto thyself wrath** against the day of wrath and revelation of the righteous judgment of God".
  • **The Final Standard:** God will judge all men: "**He will render to every man according to his deeds**".

3. Judgment by Deeds and Recompense (Romans 2:6-9)

Just as the Reprobate receives the appropriate recompense for their error, so too does every man.

CONDUCT (The Deeds) RECOMPENSE (The Judgment)
**Patient Continuance in Well Doing** (Seeking for glory and honour and immortality) **Eternal Life**
**Contentious**, and do not **obey the truth**, but obey **unrighteousness** **Indignation and Wrath**, **Tribulation and Anguish**

4. No Respect of Persons (Romans 2:9-11)

Paul introduces another key theme of the Epistle to the Romans: **Jews and Gentiles are all treated the same by God**.

  • Wrath or Peace for All: Wrath or peace is given "to the **Jew first, and also of the Gentile** (Greek)".
  • The Final Rule: "For there is **no respect of persons with God**".
