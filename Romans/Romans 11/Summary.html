Romans 9–11 • Paul's Unified Argument

No Inserted Future National Program • Faith Alone • No Boasting • Glory for All Believers

Paul's Relentless Flow (Chapters 1–11):

  • All have sinned and fall short (Rom 3:23).
  • No flesh justified by works of the law (Rom 3:20).
  • Righteousness by faith, apart from the law (Rom 3:21–22, 28).
  • The true Jew is inward, circumcision of the heart (Rom 2:28–29).
  • Not all Israel is Israel; children of promise are the seed (Rom 9:6–8).
  • Israel sought righteousness by works and stumbled (Rom 9:30–33; 10:3).
  • Zeal without knowledge; not submitted to God's righteousness (Rom 10:1–4).
  • God stretched out hands all day to disobedient people (Rom 10:21).
  • Has God cast away His people? God forbid — remnant by grace exists now (Rom 11:1–5).
  • The rest blinded/hardened (Rom 11:7–10).
  • Branches broken off for unbelief; wild branches grafted in by faith (Rom 11:17–22).
  • IF they continue not in unbelief, they may be grafted in (Rom 11:23 — conditional, individual).
  • IF Gentiles do not continue in His goodness, they too will be cut off — no boasting (Rom 11:22).
  • Blindness in part until fulness of the Gentiles (Rom 11:25).
  • And so all Israel shall be saved — the full redeemed people of God by faith (Rom 11:26; Gal 6:16).

    • No Boasting — Not of Works, Tribe, or Law:

  • Abraham had whereof to glory — but not before God (Rom 4:2).
  • By grace through faith — not of works, lest any man should boast (Eph 2:8–9).
  • Not by tribe, not by flesh, not by law-keeping — only humble belief (Rom 3:27; 4:16; Gal 3:11).
  • God’s grace alone — no quality or condition besides faith in Christ.

    • Jesus' Warnings of Cutting Off:

  • Kingdom taken and given to a nation bringing forth fruit (Matt 21:43).
  • Vineyard let to other husbandmen (Matt 21:41; Mark 12:9).
  • Children of the kingdom cast into outer darkness (Matt 8:12).
  • House left desolate (Matt 23:38).
  • Fig tree cursed and withered — fruitless Israel (Mark 11:14, 20–21).
  • Axe at the root; unfruitful trees hewn down (Matt 3:10; Luke 13:6–9).

    • The future promise: resurrection and glorification for all who believe — from every nation, kindred, tongue, and people (Rev 7:9–17; 1 Cor 15:51–54; Phil 3:20–21).

    One unified story: promise → fulfillment in Christ → one people by faith → new covenant → old done away.

    No postponed kingdom or separate national program inserted — only mercy to whosoever believes, with no boasting in flesh, works, tribe, or law.
