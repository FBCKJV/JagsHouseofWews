Dispensation

Biblical Stewardship • Household Management of the Gospel

The Greek Word Oikonomia

The word "Dispensation" in the Greek, Oikonomia literally comes from οἶκος (oikos = "house") + νόμος (nomos = "law" or "management/rule"), so it fundamentally means "household management," "administration," or "stewardship."

A steward or house manager is entrusted to oversee, protect, and faithfully distribute what belongs to the master — in this case, Paul compares his role to being entrusted with the gospel to faithfully preach and deliver it to others.

1 Corinthians 9:17

For if I do this thing willingly, I have a reward: but if against my will, a dispensation of the gospel is committed unto me.

Ephesians 3:2

If ye have heard of the dispensation of the grace of God which is given me to you-ward.

Colossians 1:25

Whereof I am made a minister, according to the dispensation of God which is given to me for you, to fulfil the word of God.

Ephesians 1:10

That in the dispensation of the fulness of times he might gather together in one all things in Christ, both which are in heaven, and which are on earth; even in him.

Paul's Use of the Term

In the three passages where Paul applies "dispensation" to himself (1 Cor 9:17, Eph 3:2, Col 1:25), he is saying he has been entrusted/stewarded with the gospel and the grace of God to faithfully preach and administer it to others.

In Ephesians 1:10, the term refers to God's own stewardship/dispensation over the entire fullness of times, gathering all things together in Christ at the end.

We all have a dispensation of the gospel — to keep the faith.

One continuous gospel in Christ from Adam through today.
God, who at sundry times and in divers manners spake in time past unto the fathers by the prophets, Hath in these last days spoken unto us by his Son... (Hebrews 1:1-2).

The difference over time was revelation of mysteries,
given by the word of God through the prophets, the preaching of Christ, and the inspired New Testament authors,
which gives us full revelation.

The Old Testament replaced by the New —
a physical nation with a spiritual one.

The "Church" is the called-out assembly of believers
at the resurrection or individual independent congregations gathering at their local NT church.
