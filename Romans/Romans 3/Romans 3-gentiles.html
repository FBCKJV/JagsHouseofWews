God's Plan for the Heathen

A Major Theme of the Book of Jonah and the Entire Old Testament

The Lesson of Jonah

The heathen are not a lost cause and should not be written off as inevitably doomed.
Already in Jonah 1, a ship full of pagan sailors feared the LORD exceedingly, offered a sacrifice unto the LORD, and made vows after seeing His power.
Jonah 1:16
In Jonah 3, the entire city of Nineveh repented on a scale unparalleled elsewhere in Scripture.
Jonah 3:5–10
The Word of God is powerful — its ability to change hearts and minds should never be underestimated.
Christians must not pick and choose whom to evangelize. The command is clear:
“Go ye into all the world, and preach the gospel to every creature.”
Mark 16:15
If we sow the seed, God promises souls will be saved:
“He that goeth forth and weepeth, bearing precious seed, shall doubtless come again with rejoicing, bringing his sheaves with him.”
Psalm 126:6

God Always Planned to Save the Gentiles

The Mosaic Law said other nations would hear of Israel’s righteous laws and say, “Surely this great nation is a wise and understanding people.”
Deuteronomy 4:6–8
The Law allowed foreigners to be circumcised and keep the Passover — becoming full Israelites.
Exodus 12:48
This naturalization of strangers is spelled out again in Ezekiel.
Ezekiel 47:22–23
Solomon prayed that foreigners from far countries would come, pray toward the temple, and know the name of the LORD.
2 Chronicles 6:32–33
God’s house shall be called “a house of prayer for all people.”
Isaiah 56:7
Peter confirmed in the New Testament: “In every nation he that feareth him, and worketh righteousness, is accepted with him.”
Acts 10:35

Named Gentile Believers in the KJV Old Testament
(Genesis 12 onward – not descendants of Jacob)

1. Melchizedek

King of Salem, priest of the most high God (possibly a pre-incarnate Christ).

Genesis 14:18–20
2. Abimelech, King of Gerar

Acknowledged God was with Abraham & Isaac; made covenants in the name of the LORD.

Genesis 20–21, 26
3. Jethro (Reuel)

Midianite priest who confessed, “Now I know that the LORD is greater than all gods.”

Exodus 18:9–12
4. Rahab the Canaanite

“The LORD your God, he is God in heaven above, and in earth beneath.”

Joshua 2:11; 6:25
5. The Gibeonites

Canaanites who entered covenant with Israel and served at the tabernacle.

Joshua 9–10
6. Ruth the Moabitess

“Thy people shall be my people, and thy God my God.”

Ruth 1:16
7. The Queen of Sheba

Blessed “the LORD thy God” and glorified His name.

1 Kings 10:1–9; 2 Chronicles 9:1–8
8. Hiram, King of Tyre

Blessed “the LORD God of Israel, that made heaven and earth.”

2 Chronicles 2:12
9. Widow of Zarephath

“I know that … the word of the LORD in thy mouth is truth.”

1 Kings 17:24
10. Naaman the Syrian

“I know that there is no God in all the earth, but in Israel” — vowed to worship only YHWH.

2 Kings 5:15–17
11. Nebuchadnezzar

“I Nebuchadnezzar praise and extol and honour the King of heaven … whose dominion is an everlasting dominion.”

Daniel 4:34–37 (see also 2:47; 3:28–29)
12. Job

Righteous Gentile from Uz who feared God and eschewed evil.

Book of Job
13. Cyrus, King of Persia

Called God’s anointed and shepherd; proclaimed the LORD gave him all kingdoms and commanded the temple rebuilt.

2 Chronicles 36:22–23; Isaiah 44:28–45:4
Other Notable Mentions:
• The heathen sailors who sacrificed to the LORD (Jonah 1:14–16)
• The entire city of Nineveh that repented (Jonah 3)
• Many Persians who “became Jews” for fear of the LORD (Esther 8:17)
God is willing that NONE should perish — the heathen are NOT beyond the reach of His mercy and the power of His Word!
