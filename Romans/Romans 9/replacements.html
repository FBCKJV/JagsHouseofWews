God's Pattern in Scripture

Choosing the Weak, the Foolish, the Younger, the Unexpected

“But God hath chosen the foolish things of the world to confound the wise;
and God hath chosen the weak things of the world to confound the things which are mighty;
And base things of the world, and things which are despised, hath God chosen, yea, and things which are not, to bring to nought things that are:
That no flesh should glory in his presence.”

(1 Corinthians 1:27–29 KJV)

Throughout Scripture we see the same repeated pattern: God deliberately chooses what man considers weak, foolish, or unlikely,
so that His power and purpose are unmistakably displayed and all boasting is excluded.

This theme of God choosing the weak, foolish, and unexpected runs throughout the entire Bible.

• First wilderness generation > Second generation (entered land – Num/Deut)

• Gideon > Midianite oppressors (Judg 6–8)
• Jephthah > His elder brothers & Ammon (Judg 11)

• Ahaziah/Athaliah > Joash preserved (2 Kings)

• Abiathar > Zadok (priestly line)

• Dry bones > Revived army (Ezek 37)

• High places > Lowly exalted (teaching)

The Bible consistently shows God delighting to use what the world counts as nothing
so that all the glory goes to Him alone and no flesh can boast.
The first shall be last and the last shall be first.
