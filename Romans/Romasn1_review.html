Author: Paul

  • Formerly Saul of Tarsus — zealous Pharisee who persecuted the church
  • Radically converted on the road to Damascus

The Church at Rome

  • The only major church Paul wrote to that he did not personally plant
  • Mixed congregation: believing Jews + former Roman pagans
  • Already famous throughout the world for their faith (Rom 1:8)

Major Themes of Romans

  • Salvation is by grace through faith alone
  • Gentile believers are full and equal members of God’s people
  • Practical implications of the gospel in daily life

Literary Style: Diatribe

  • A persuasive logical argument using if/then statements, hypothetical objections (interlocutor), and conversational tone

Romans 1:1-17 — Greeting & Theme

“For I am not ashamed of the gospel of Christ: for it is the power of God unto salvation to every one that believeth; to the Jew first, and also to the Greek. For therein is the righteousness of God revealed from faith to faith: as it is written, The just shall live by faith.”
— Romans 1:16-17

Romans 1:18-32 — The Wrath of God Revealed

  • God’s wrath is presently revealed from heaven against all ungodliness
  • Men are without excuse — God’s eternal power and divine nature clearly seen
  • They suppressed the truth and refused to retain God in their knowledge
  • Therefore God gave them up / gave them over (3 times) to a reprobate mind
