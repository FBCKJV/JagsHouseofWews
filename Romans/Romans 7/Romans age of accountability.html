Romans 7:7-13

The Age of Accountability • The Holy Law Reveals Sin

7-13

What shall we say then? Is the law sin? God forbid. Nay, I had not known sin, but by the law: for I had not known lust, except the law had said, Thou shalt not covet.

But sin, taking occasion by the commandment, wrought in me all manner of concupiscence. For without the law sin was dead.

For I was alive without the law once: but when the commandment came, sin revived, and I died. And the commandment, which was ordained to life, I found to be unto death. For sin, taking occasion by the commandment, deceived me, and by it slew me.

Wherefore the law is holy, and the commandment holy, and just, and good.

Key Principle from Romans:
Where there is no law, sin is not imputed (Romans 4:15; 5:13).

Without knowledge of God's commandment, there is no conscious transgression — and therefore no imputation of sin.

The Age of Accountability

Young children and the mentally challenged who lack the capacity to understand God's moral law are not condemned for sin in the same way as those who knowingly rebel.

God judges according to the light received — and where moral understanding is absent, He does not impute guilt.

After the death of the infant son born to David and Bathsheba, David declared with confidence:
“I shall go to him, but he shall not return to me” (2 Samuel 12:23)

—expressing his assurance that he would one day be reunited with his child in heaven.
Matthew 18:3

And said, Verily I say unto you, Except ye be converted, and become as little children, ye shall not enter into the kingdom of heaven.

Jesus holds up the humble, trusting nature of little children as the pattern for entering the kingdom — affirming their innocence and safe standing before God.
