Romans Chapter 16

Historical Context of the Named Saints • Real People • Real Faith • Real Ministry

Phoebe (vv. 1-2)

A prominent Christian woman from Cenchrea (port of Corinth). Paul calls her a servant/deacon (diakonos) of the church and a succourer/patron (prostatis) of many, including himself. She likely carried this letter to Rome and was a woman of means who supported traveling ministers.

Prisca (Priscilla) & Aquila (vv. 3-5)

A married Jewish couple, tentmakers by trade. Expelled from Rome by Emperor Claudius (A.D. 49) along with other Jews. They hosted Paul in Corinth, traveled with him to Ephesus, and hosted a house church in both Ephesus and Rome. They risked their lives for Paul and instructed Apollos more accurately in the faith. Priscilla is often named first, showing her prominent role.

Other Key Individuals

  • Epaenetus — Paul’s beloved; the first convert to Christ in the province of Asia (Achaia).
  • Mary — Worked very hard for the Roman believers.
  • Andronicus & Junia — Paul’s Jewish relatives (likely a couple); fellow prisoners with Paul; became Christians before him and were well-known among the apostles.
  • Ampliatus, Urbanus, Stachys, Apelles — Beloved in the Lord and fellow workers.
  • Household of Aristobulus & Herodion — Likely connected to prominent households; Herodion was Paul’s relative.
  • Tryphena & Tryphosa, Persis — Women who “laboured much in the Lord.”
  • Rufus & his mother — Rufus “chosen in the Lord”; his mother had been like a mother to Paul.
  • Asyncritus, Phlegon, Hermas, Patrobas, Hermes, Philologus, Julia, Nereus & his sister, Olympas — Groups of believers meeting in house churches; many were slaves or freedpeople.

Overall Context (vv. 17-20)

Paul warns the church to mark and avoid those who cause divisions and offences contrary to the doctrine they had learned. Many in this list had endured persecution (expulsion under Claudius) yet remained faithful. The Roman church was diverse — Jews and Gentiles, men and women, slaves and free — all united in Christ through house churches.
