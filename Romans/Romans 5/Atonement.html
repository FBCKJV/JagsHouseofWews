Atonement & Reconciliation
in Romans

The Word “Atonement”

Appears 70 times in the KJV
69 times in the Old Testament
Once in the New Testament

70 — God’s number of divine completion
and perfection

Romans 5:11 (KJV)

“And not only so, but we also joy in God
through our Lord Jesus Christ,
by whom we have now received
the atonement.”

This “atonement” translates the Greek
katallagē (καταλλαγή),
meaning reconciliation —

the restoration of a broken relationship,
bringing sinful humanity back into favor
and peace with a holy God.

Modern translations miss the mark.

Key Verses

Romans 5:10
“For if, when we were enemies,
we were reconciled to God by the death of his Son,
much more, being reconciled,
we shall be saved by his life.”

Romans 5:11
“...by whom we have now received
the atonement [reconciliation].”

Atonement and Reconciliation
are synonymous:

God’s purchase of the sins of the world
by the blood of Christ

This brings the offer of peace with God
through the gift of salvation
— a gift that must be
received by faith
